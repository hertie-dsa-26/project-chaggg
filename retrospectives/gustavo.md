# My retrospectives on building the project

## **Major contributions**

### Problem definition
During the initial discussions, we couldn’t decide on a research question. We started working on data cleaning and visualizations prior an agreement was reached. Data exploration allowed us to realise what could be done with the data at hand. When time came to choose the ML-algorithm to be developed, I came up with an alternative model. This one was feasible, considering time restrictions, and executable with our current knowledge and toolkit. In fact, our knn-logistic ridge regression algorithm does not need additional information to the City of Chicago dataframe and is (relatively) easy to follow and implement at the ‘beginner level.’

### Data cleaning
When working with visualizations, I realised that our approach was not correct for the actual structure of our dataframe. We then had to choose a different id variable and collapse the dataframe accordingly. This gave us a correct interpretation of the data and, thus, accurate and realistic results.

## **Eine kleine aber wichtige Erfahrung**

### Teamwork with international peers
Before coming to Hertie, I worked for almost eight years at Mexico’s antitrust agency. I am used to working in, leading and coordinating teams. However, I had never worked with people from different countries (and cultural backgrounds) to build a product. I found it interesting to note the differences on what we understand for _punctuality_, _leadership_, _clearness_ or _responsibility_. But also how we deal with stress, how we express disagreement or joy for an accomplishment and how we let others know how we feel. I don’t mean to blame or criticise anyone really. My intention is to document a note-to-self: diversity goes beyond the use of pronouns. And, as a part of the human experience, it deserves to be embraced too.
